Quick Start Guide
  • Explore: Click any district to load its data. Click 'Show Total Lebanon' to see the national summary.
  • Filter: Use the 'Year' and 'Map Theme' buttons to control the data shown on the map and in the panels.
  • Analyze: Get a pre-calculated 'AI Policy Insight' or use the 'Ask AI' chat for a live response.
  • View Details: All analysis panels are collapsible. Click any panel's title to open or close it.
  • Customize: Drag and drop the analysis panels by their title bars to reorder your layout.

What is 'AI Policy Insight'?

Details: An automated analysis that highlights the primary demographic challenge or opportunity for the selected district.

How it Works: This insight is not generated live. Key metrics for each district were fed to a Generative AI, and the analysis was stored in the GeoJSON file.

Ask AI (Live Analysis)

About this AI Feature

This AI combines its general knowledge (to understand language) with the live data provided from this dashboard to answer your questions.

It is strictly instructed to NOT use any external facts or data in its answer.

Its answers are based exclusively on:

  • Population structure (by age)
  • Dependency Ratios
  • Growth trends (2015-2030)
  • National averages for comparison

If you ask about other topics (e.g., unemployment, poverty), it will state that the information is not available.

What is 'AI Trend Classification'?

Details: These tags (e.g., "Aging", "Youth Bulge") are pre-calculated based on a statistical model of a district's demographic share and growth rate.

In short: This filter instantly highlights all districts on the map that share the same significant demographic profile.

Dependency Ratio

What is 'Dependency Ratio'?

Calculation: (Total Youth + Total Elderly) ÷ Total Working-Age × 100

Interpretation: A ratio of 60% means there are 60 "dependent" people for every 100 people of Working-Age.

In short: A key metric measuring the economic pressure on the productive population. A lower ratio is generally more favorable.
